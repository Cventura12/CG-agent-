# Arbor 10/10 Execution Plan

Last updated: 2026-03-21

## What 10/10 Actually Means

For Arbor, 10/10 does not mean:

- more pages
- more animations
- more AI language
- more features stacked on top of weak ones

It means:

1. the core workflow is undeniable
2. the product is trusted by contractors
3. the voice layer is dependable, not just impressive
4. the office always knows what changed, what is waiting, and what goes out next
5. the system clearly protects revenue and follow-through

The standard is:

> A contractor can rely on Arbor daily without wondering whether the system missed the important thing.

## The 10/10 Product Standard

### 1. Core workflow: 10/10

The flagship flow should feel effortless:

1. field update comes in
2. Arbor catches it
3. Arbor extracts what changed
4. the review item appears with context
5. a draft quote or follow-up is already prepared
6. the owner approves it
7. the job record updates immediately
8. the system can show that outcome later

10/10 means:

- no ambiguity
- no missing state
- no loose ends
- no hidden failures

### 2. Voice: 10/10

Voice should feel like a real operating advantage.

10/10 means:

- live calls feel conversational
- questioning feels contractor-specific
- interruptions are handled naturally
- low-confidence calls degrade safely
- operator review is fast and obvious
- every call leaves a useful operational artifact

### 3. Jobs: 10/10

Jobs should be the operating truth center.

10/10 means every job answers:

- what changed
- what is unresolved
- what is blocked
- what was promised
- what needs approval
- what goes out next

### 4. Contractor trust: 10/10

10/10 trust means:

- the product never feels vague
- failure states are visible
- audit trails are clear
- operators can recover from uncertainty
- customer-facing actions are controlled

### 5. ROI clarity: 10/10

The product should prove value inside the workflow.

10/10 means it can show:

- quote deltas caught
- missed scope surfaced
- follow-ups prevented from slipping
- time from field update to review
- revenue protected or recovered

## Current Reality

Current rough state:

- problem clarity: 9/10

## The Rule For Getting To 10/10

Do not widen.
Do not decorate.
Do not over-automate before the workflow is trustworthy.

Instead:

1. tighten the main loop
2. harden the runtime
3. simplify operator decisions
4. prove money saved

## What Must Become Excellent

### A. Queue quality

Queue items need to be right often enough that the office trusts them.

Needed:

- clearer extraction quality
- better urgency detection
- stronger manual-review states
- cleaner connection between queue items and jobs

If queue quality is weak, the whole product feels noisy.

### B. Draft quality

Draft quotes and follow-ups need to feel genuinely helpful.

Needed:

- better scope extraction
- better job context carry-over
- more trustworthy value suggestions
- cleaner post-approval state

If the draft is weak, the agent feels cosmetic.

### C. Voice quality

Voice needs to become the thing people talk about after using it.

Needed:

- stronger intent routing
- better contractor-specific questioning
- better interruption handling
- cleaner operator call review
- safer low-confidence fallback

### D. Job truth

Jobs cannot just be organized.
They need to feel current.

Needed:

- unresolved items summary
- latest change summary
- blocked next step
- customer-facing state
- cleaner timeline of approved actions

## What To Stop Doing

To reach 10/10, we should stop:

- adding duplicate explanation instead of proof
- widening into generic contractor software
- shipping decorative analytics before trust surfaces
- overexplaining agent intelligence instead of showing operational outcomes
- treating voice as marketing before it is operationally dependable
