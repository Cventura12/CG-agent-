# Arbor Fundraising Product Narrative

Last updated: 2026-03-21

## The Thesis

Contractors do not just lose money because they estimate badly.

They lose money because the job changes faster than the office can keep the record current.

The field communicates through:

- calls
- texts
- voice notes
- photos
- uploads
- ad hoc follow-up

Important changes live inside that mess.

That means:

- extra work gets done and never billed
- owner requests get buried
- paperwork gets missed
- follow-up depends on memory
- the office is often operating on an older version of the job

Arbor is built to close that gap.

## The Product

Arbor is an agentic operations system for field contractors.

It watches inbound field communication, isolates what changed, surfaces it for review, and prepares the next operational step before revenue slips through.

The core loop is:

1. capture inbound signal
2. understand what changed
3. surface a decision
4. prepare the next action
5. write the result back into the job record

This is not a CRM.
It is not scheduling software.
It is not a generic AI wrapper.

It is an execution layer for the messy space between the field and the office.

## Why Now

Contractors already have more communication than they can reliably operationalize.

They do not need another inbox.
They do not need another dashboard.

They need a system that can:

- catch the change
- preserve the context
- put the next decision in front of the office
- keep the job record aligned with reality

AI is now finally good enough to read messy operational inputs and turn them into structured reviewable work.

That makes this timing real.

## Why This Problem Matters

This is not a convenience problem.

It is a margin problem.

When a contractor misses a change, stalls follow-up, or forgets what was promised:

- supplements go out late
- revised work never gets billed
- jobs drift
- customer trust drops
- owner attention gets wasted on reconstruction instead of decisions

Arbor is designed to recover that lost operational value.

## The Product Wedge

The most compelling wedge is:

> field change -> review -> draft quote or follow-up -> approval -> logged back to the job

That is where Arbor becomes undeniable.

Instead of trying to be everything a contractor uses, Arbor is focusing first on the most expensive communication gap in the business.

## What Makes It Agentic

Arbor is not just displaying data.

It is:

- watching inbound communication
- extracting structured meaning
- identifying unresolved work
- preparing draft outcomes
- keeping state across the workflow
- writing decisions back into the operating record

The human stays in control of customer-facing actions, which is exactly right for this market.

That makes Arbor meaningfully agentic while remaining trustworthy.
